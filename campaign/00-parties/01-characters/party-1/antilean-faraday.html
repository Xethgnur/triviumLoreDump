History

Named at birth Ant-Lion-in-the-Summer-Flowers, Antilean travelled with a Tabaxi troop that wanders up and down the continent of Arcadia. The troop itself is lax and free for expression among other troop members, and believe in the powers of discovery and bartering. Antilean served his troop well as a hunter, meeting his long term partner during a botched hunt, where she had saved him from a large beast. In time, the tables were turned, and Antilean saved her. And together, they declared their undying love for one another, and raised kids over the course of a few years.

However. A new interest peeked Antilean's curiosity. Visiting a Rock Gnome settlement, new objects never before seen that work in ways never imagined had caused Antilean to become obsessed. Enough that he would tinker in the wee hours of the night, while his wife slept with the children to comfort them. At one point, when the troop passed by a Dwarven settlement, a master artisan in the ways of smithing intricate items was intrigued with Antilean as much as Antilean was intrigued with him. He would take him in as a strange apprentice, to register under Dwarven Apprenticeship and Certification, and become a master artisan like the Dwarf himself. But he would have to leave his family. Antilean promised Serran that he would come back to her, with crats and riches and comforts like no other. Serran was suppotive if no hurt, but supportive.

Years passed, gruelling training, mounds of paperwork, moulds made and moulds lost, fur singed and fur grown; Antilean became a certified Dwarven Grade Artisan in Small Item Smithing, and his experimental creation, the hand cannon, impressed his Dwarven judges with such fine craftmanship from a novice crafter. Now, it was up to Antilean to go back out into the world, and develop his technique, though under Non-Disclosure of Dwarven learning and crafting techniques, which, by Dwarven Law of Written Stone, will not and cannot be contractually shared outside those of the Pact of Written Stone. Years passed, investments made and failed, contacts saved and lost; and a letter from Sarren arrives in the postal letting Antilean know that it is clear his passions laid elsewhere, and not with those he once loved. She called off the marriage, and they were officially considered separated.

Still determined to prove he can make it big in the economic world of venture capitalism, Antilean went to the Capital City of Vasaaros, the perfect place to trade. Someone there would seek his contraption that fired small bullets with explosions in quick succession. Someone there would kickstart his business... Though, perhaps not in the way he had expected...
