Session 15

Following Session 14:

The session starts with Duan Ping renouncing his own noble house, and removes himself from the candidacy for the throne of the emperor, and takes up the mantle of kings guard which disallows him to have children. Vimack Badger, Ronpip Thimble-bimble, Evan Escence, and Lorellai attend this announcement, and it is solemn energy across the public crowd. Evan tries to meet with Duan, and Ronpip hands him a short sword. Evan meets with Duan, and is reeled by the announcement. Duan explains that this was his intent the whole time, and though his mother is unhappy, she is in charge of the noble house but nothing more will damage their reputation. He wishes to bring back honor to his name, and seek justice against those who damaged the reputation of his house. They discuss who is left in the running, and Duan confides that they will work more together when Evan is emperor. Evan is taken aback, and hesitantly agrees.

He returns to the party, and together they agree to investigate the Cathedral of Stars. There, they discover that the Cathedral is a conglomerate of multiple gods together, revealing that Arcaedia allows for varying worship of many gods together. The Cathedral of Stars is very large, filled with people, and at the center is a massive armillary depicting the movement of the sun, the two moons, and stars or planes of existence in balance among themselves, spinning and orbiting in a complex series of movement. Vimack spots a Judge of Arcaedia and he asks him a hypothetical question about how to legally accuse a individual or group of higher social status. The Judge of Arcaedia relays hesitantly that he would probably want physical proof to identify those he wants to accuse, and states to take it up with the nobles, not with the Brotherhood of Judges or Sisterhood associated with them.

The party continues to look around, and Vimack is unable to see the high priestess of Cathedral of Stars or anyone else from the fight. Evan thinks back and remembers her name is Priestess Yurreina Paramoure. Lorellai uses her divine sense and immediately knows that she is a space that is protected by the Holy version of the Hallow spell, and detects a mix of more good than evil. Evan goes to the nearest priest of the Cathedral of Stars and asks where "sister" Yereina is, and he is corrected that she is "High" Priestess Yurreina Paramoure. The priest says she is not here, but her son Derek Paramoure is at the feeding house, feeding the beggars, and Atoli Paramoure is caring for the children and orphans of those visiting the temple. Vimack asks if there are more children of hers and the priest mentionsAlemhon Paramoure but that he is "rebellious". The party decides to speak to which ever they run into first. They run into Derek, who is in a kitchen dedicated towards feeding those who cannot feed or house themselves. He is not wearing the same Cathedral of Stars robes, but he has a well decorated scarf to make him stand out. Vimack approaches him and leads to Evan to ask the questions. Evan asks again for Priestess Yurreina Paramoure, but Derek says that she is not at the Cathedral of Stars, implying she has not actually worked here, especially for a length of time.

While the party is talking to Derek, an old lady comes up to Vimack, offering him a folded flower of parchment. She tells him to open it. Vimack opens the flower, and finds five gold coins, and a note written in it.

The note reads: "Tell your brother 'Thank You' for the advice. Here is his commission. And your reward for being a Liar. The Beak P.S. - You are always welcome to a Leatherback Tunn"

Vimack is stabbed by a poisoned dagger while he is momentarily distracted by the coins and note. No one has noticed this happen until Vimack suddenly reels in pain and sudden weakness that courses through his body. The last thing Vimack sees is the old hag who stabbed him smile with blackened teeth, and she seems to disappear into mist and shadow, but she is gone before the party could have seen her. Derek speaks out and asks if Vimack is okay in genuine concern, but Ronpip is now alarmed, and lashes out at Derek. Evan tries to defend Ronpip's sudden move change. Derek moves in to help, but Ronpip demands that Derek keeps his hands where Ronpip can see them. Vimack does his best to say that it was not Derek, but Ronpip is on edge. Lorellai uses her Lay on Hands to neutralize the poison within him, and heal him a bit. Ronpip sees that there are traces of black ichor on the healed portions of the wound. Vimack begins to rage, and the area becomes statically charged. Vimack is about to read the letter out loud, but Ronpip stops him. Ronpip apologizes to Derek for his outburst, and Vimack confesses that certain business has caught up to the party. Derek would like the party would leave due to the outburst.

The party leaves the facility, and Vimack hands the gold over to Ronpip as the letter implies such. Ronpip wishes to know where the nearest Leatherback Tunn is and Evan recalls the nearest one is definitely outside the temple district. The party questions if he really wants to resolve that now, but Ronpip reluctantly accepts they should seek out Atoli instead. The search for Atoli and come across a very roughly made daycare, and they spot a few women with headdresses, one with the same scarf as Derek. Evan tells Vimack if a kid offers him a flower, to not accept it and Ronpip agrees. Vimack is disappointed but concedes to the notion. Vimack steps over the fence and greets the kids, and they are completely enamored. Ronpip, meanwhile, is on watch, paranoid of any ne'er-do-wells potentially here to bother them again. Vimack proceeds to play with the children, allowing Evan to speak to Atoli though she remains busy with other children. Evan proceeds to ask about Priestess Yurreina Paramoure, but she says that they could find her at the estate, stating its odd that since she was blessed by a god that its odd she is never around. Evan inquires what god she is blessed by, but Atoli does not believe it matters. Evan is confused, as there are clerics and paladins about, but she reminds Evan that not all clerics are blessed, they simply do clerical work. Evan asks about Alemhon, but Atoli does not speak well of him in relation to the temple, saying he often runs off to do whatever he wants, stating if she had her mother's divining powers she might actually be able to find him. Lorellai relates with Atoli and has a conversation with her about her visions and abilities, and Atoli seems interested deeply, especially in the fact that she wears armor and has weapons. Atoli begins to call the children back and Vimack releases the kids from his entertainment, covered in child gunk. Evan uses Metaphycisian to peal away the gunk from Vimack and clean him of it.

The party leaves the temple district and proceed to take a detour towards the amphitheater, hoping things are left as is. And they discover nothing has been taken down quite yet, but the bodies of their oppressors are gone. Vimack lifts Ronpip and Evan and Lorellai to investigate the booths the nobles would have sat in to watch the fight between Escence and Urie. In Paramoure's booth, there appears to be a marking stained into the wood. Ronpip sees that it offers a very dim, faint aura of Conjuration. Lorellai recognizes it as demonic sigil of sorts. Evan channels psionic energy into his throat, and is able to read any language and speak to any creature in their language. Ronpip asks for paper, and Vimack hoists himself into the booth. Evan panics and tells him to be careful, and Ronpip is taken aback at how clear Evan's speech suddenly is. Vimack notices the sigil is stained into the plank of wood the floor is made from. Vimack pulls out his crowbar, and begins to rip out the plank. Lorellai is very concerned and wonders if this is a good idea, and Evan says its okay, he allows it, misunderstanding what Lorellai is actually concerned with. In response to Vimack, the booth begins to creek and waver, and begins to collapse now that a piece of the floor that was supporting it is being torn free from its nails. Evan gets piled on to into the collapsed booth while everyone else managed to roll out of the booth and to safety. They pull Evan out who cleans himself from the dust of the rubble. Evan wishes to go the estate of House Paramoure but Vimack proposes they speak to Duan Ping first, in order to gather further support.

The party returns the nobles district, and discusses just how much support they could acquire. Ronpip suggests talking to Chevelle, since they are one of the few Lorellai detected good from. They head to the Noble House of Foo first. The meet with Duan Ping in a familiar way, though more forward facing and less to the side with secrecy. They present the demon sigil on the wood plank to him and inquire if this is evidence to accuse Paramoure, but Duan Ping is not convinced this would be enough. Duan Ping also reveals he has no possible clue what Yereina's clerical domain would be as his culture does not dabble in religious magic, at least not to his knowledge. He offers a measured advice, and sends the party on the way with assurance that the party might need more to persuade the houses Evan has support with.

The party decides to return to Cathedral of Stars to poke around a little more. The temple is pretty full with sermon being spoken about a god of the harvest, with many merchants, farmers, and artisans listening and praying for a good season next year. Evan almost decides to leave and Ronpip protests wondering if Evan is concerned about his business having supply, but notices his father is there. He waves and calls out his father, and his father is caught by surprise, interrupting the sermon. Vimack meanwhile has been searching for a physician or medical ward. He finds a doctor and asks him about his stab wound. He gives it a look over and Vimack tries to face wave his party to ask the doctor things while he's here. They begin to ask him friendly questions, and then lean in to get answers for potentially why the Cathedral of Stars might need more shipments of medicines as of late. Ronpip glances around, and realizes that every patient has a Thrall's Neckpiece on their person. The group starts putting things together and realize there is potentially a cult involved in the distribution of the necklace. The party wishes to potentially talk with the patients, but the doctor has resignations. Lorellai states she is not afraid of one of them potentially shivs her. She takes a look at one of the Thrall's Neckpieces on a patient and immediately gets bad vibes from the item. Lorellai speaks to a patient who tells her he recieved the Thrall's Neckpiece from a friendly high elf, and that he and his family are sick. Ronpip has an educational discussion with the doctor, learning he attended the colleges across Arcaedia, and he has visited the other countries and kingdoms except Hvergelmir. He inquires about cadavers and the dead, and the doctor mentions there's a crematorium on the opposite side of the district, away from the Cathedral of Stars. Lorellai cures the mans wounds, and he feels a little better. Ronpip mentions there's a crematorium that the doctor mentioned, and the party heads to it. A graveyard surrounds the crematorium, and Ronpip notices that the crematorium was used as recently as last night. The party tries to determine what to do next, and an old man pulling a cart via a donkey is headed towards the crematorium. Ronpip greets him and he seems really keen on burning bodies. They have a discussion about how a lot of people die in the city and outside of the city, and Vimack is getting antsy with the dead bodies in the cart.

Vimack thinks they should break into the Paramoure estate, and gather as much information with subterfuge instead of asking around. Evan wants to scope out the outside before they break in. The party realizes Paul McCoy has been with them the whole time, remaining in the back and at a distance unless they needed to travel by canal waterway. They ask him for his advice, and he suggests the direct approach. Ronpip highly agrees, while everyone else starts agreeing as well that their attempts be sneaky and subtle have failed miserably.

They arrive at the estate of Paramoure, and it is open gates to a massive estate with multiple buildings within the property. There are no guards, and lots of religious iconography about, including a temple within the estate walls. Inside they find a strange, unidentifiable statue of what might be a goddess, with a diamond shaped hole in her chest. While in the temple, they see a mausoleum at the back of the estate. Ronpip is curious about the diamond shaped hole in the statues chest, and as soon as he gets high enough, sees only that it is carved through perfectly smoothed but otherwise unassuming. The party, no longer interested in sticking their hands into holes, head to the mausoleum. From what the party can see, this is where the house contains their ancestorial ashes. On the back of the wall, there appears to be an inscription in Celestial. Lorellai recognizes it, and says that it is Celestial writing stating: As above, so below; as within, so without. Ronpip uses his Detect Magic and see the inscription is magical and illusory in nature. Lorellai speaks the words in Celestial, and the floor drops and reveals a stairway that leads downwards. Celestial script glows with the same inscription, dimly lighting the way downward.

The party enter the catacombs with caution. Once they reach the bottom, the words stop providing light and instead a shimmering stone from the ceiling is dimly providing the light. The party continues deeper into the catacombs, and Vimack can visualize that it is following the same flagstone path leading to the temple is the same as the layout they are following. Eventually, the party reaches deep enough to hear some low chanting coming from ahead. Based on Vimack's understanding of the layout, they are in the under-temple, which appears to be a reversal of the temple above. And the statue from the temple on the surface is now on the ceiling, though now in an appearance of something more akin to a bandaged and dominated version, with a similar hole in the chest of a perfect diamond. The party has stumbled upon a sermon among cultist followers, with the leader in leather bandaged wear and blackened feathers aimed upwards for a more sharpened appearance. She speaks about war and turmoil, and helping the high elves win the invasion is the key to greater purpose. She wields an maul with complex arcane inscription written upon it. Evan confirms in a whisper that Priestess Yurreina Paramoure is most certainly guilty.

The chanting stops. The cultists rise and face the party. Ten of them are average robed humans, and three hulking members are standing before Priestess Yurreina Paramoure. She flourishes her maul, and proceeds to tell her cultists to attack the party.

Battle commences in Session 16.
