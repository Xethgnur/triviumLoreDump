Session 17

Following Session 16

Ronpip Thimble-bimble, Lorellai, and Vimack Badger, now joined by Antilean Faraday, have left Vasaaros for Phocellis to follow up on Lorellai's dream quest.

Along the way, Antilean notices that Ropip has had some methodical exercises throughout the trip, practicing his disciplinary techniques passed down from being a veteran soldier. Antilean waves down Ronpip, and asks him if he would like to try out his Big Iron, the handcannon he used a few days back to demonstrate to a noble. Ronpip is a mix of intrigue and hesitancy, which Ronpip can feel its heft and personally see its design. Antilean begins to explain how it works and how to use, and sets up a target for him to try out with live fire. Antilean calls over Vimack to take a look at both he weapon's demonstration and Ronpip's potential skill with it. Vimack proceeds to them, though is nervous and suspect of Antilean. Ronpip aims the Big Iron, and first shot makes a bull's eye on the target. Vimack, in reaction to the sound and strike against the target, goes into a rage, channeling lightning through his body as he questions what just played out in innocent misunderstanding. He asks Ronpip what is the object and its "devil magic" and Ronpip points out its pretty normal physics and alchemy, and demonstrates by firing an Eldritch Blast, that such a feat is closer to what Vimack is describing, as the Tool of War comes into his hand and emulates the shape of Big Iron, though clearly is just a hunk of metal with no moving parts. Vimack, enraged that these explanations are not fitting together in his head, builds enough electricity to then strike the target with the latent lightning building in his body. Then, Ronpip takes a moment to notice that the Tool of War is in the form of a hand cannon and questions it, and it replies that this form is the same as the "weapon" in his other hand. Antilean takes interest in Ronpip's Tool of War but Ronpip shows him the Tool of War in the hand cannon form, and questions how the device functions. Vimack really wants to see Ronpip hand the Tool of War over to Antilean, knowing it has immense weight when not in Ronpip's hands. Ronpip is hesitant and asks the Tool of War for consent as well as warning Antilean that he does not know what he is in for. However, Antilean is too intrigued by the device to consider what that means. Ronpip hands over the Tool of War, and immediately, Antilean's hands drop from the weight and Antilean panics from the weight, demanding Ronpip take it before he drops it. Ronpip easily lifts it out of his hands like it weighs a little more than nothing, while Vimack and Dorizoul roll about the ground giggling at the show. Lorellai also wants to try an finds she can't hold the item either. Antilean shares an idiom that he finds best describes the situation.

On another day of camping, Antilean manages to explain his craft to Vimack; and Vimack, having a history of blacksmithing, is much more interested in Antilean and his artisanship. Vimack recalls a shield he smithed, a kite shield he had to forge a phoenix insignia designed within that he was commissioned from within Thimbleville as a memorial present to someone who had passed. The group of them show off each other's tools of their trades. Antilean unlocks and unfolds his wagon, showing off his accoutrements that fold into the limited work space. Vimack dumps out his Thimbleville made smithing tools, which Antilean recognizes their origin for their unique design. Ronpip also has a set of Tinkerer's Tools, on the off chance he decides to tinker with something. Through this, they find a better point of commonality and comradery.

While traveling, Antilean calls the group over and wishes to tell them a tale within his life. He talks about when he was still with his caravan, he and a few brothers and sisters needed to trap some dangerous creatures. They managed to track the creatures down, and split up to converge on the creatures with tactile strategy, but the creatures found Antilean's partner, and Antilean had to stand his ground against the creatures until help could arrive. And that's how he met Serran. Vimack is confused, and Antilean explains further, including kids he had with Serran.

Just as the tale of Antilean's life comes to a close, Vimack and Antilean notice that down the road, there are two conspicuous bushes on either side of the travel road, and a humanoid has another over his shoulder, appearing to try and help him cross the road. Vimack glances at the bushes some more, but sees nothing; Antilean recognizes this ploy from his own travels. Vimack speaks up and points out someone is hurt, but Antilean tries to stop him, knowing that something is very likely going on. Vimack rides ahead to call out to the injured folk ahead, asking if they need assistance. Ronpip and Antilean prepare a level of defenses between them, and Ronpip keeps up behind Vimack. Lorellai joins up with Antilean. Vimack dismounts, and moves forward to help them out.

As Vimack is approaching, the two playing injured and helper stand up straight and pull out heavy crossbows, aiming at him and the party. Four more men from each bush hope out, with heavy crossbows as well. And four more lift up a false carpet of grass from the sides of the road, and aim their crossbows at the party from behind them. Surrounded, a mustard yellow Tiefling with pitch black horns struts out from one of the bushes into the road to lead his merry highway men, who are all wearing green and grey leather armor, showing their coordination and unison. The Tiefling attempts to instruct the group on what to do in their situation, but Vimack is much more concerned about the potentially injured man from earlier. He argues between Vimack and Ronpip on what the party should do versus what it is the party is doing, leading to chaotic banter between the two groups.

Vimack is unsure what to do, asking his brother, Ronpip, what to do in this situation, but their brotherly banter leads to mixed instructions. Antilean is also confused and vexed by the current events, unsure if he should continue to keep a hand on Big Iron or what an alternative might look like. Vimack looks around him, and at first believes that he is outmatched from numbers alone; but after bantering between the tiefling, his brother, and himself, he notices that these men are definitely not actually prepared for the party, such as people's aim being slightly off, the smugness of their leader, and on a deeper level the flaws of the strategy these highwaymen are using begin to show more and more. Ronpip has had enough time to assess them, and he determines he could probably flick them and they would keel over. Antilean keeps making threats, and Ronpip tries to keep him calm and to wait things out for now. The Tiefling clarifies that all they want are their goods in the wagon and anything they have on hand, a simple request in his words. Ronpip asks how much they will pay for their stuff, and the Tiefling says they will recieve the party's things for free.

Antilean, being a coin-minded marketer, ain't having that, and shoots a guy. Vimack goes into a rage and tries to trip the tiefling leader, but he manages to jump over the flat of the blade. Ronpip summons the Tool of War as a lance in preparation for a fight, and fires an Eldritch Blast through it, striking the rapier in the tiefling's hand and obliteration the rapier, its guard, and the sword hand of the tiefling. The tiefling has to take a moment to understand what had just happened, and begins to scream in pain and caress his hand. His comrades begin to panic, unsure what to do, and Antilean continues to threaten them. They throw their weapons down and apologize, surrendering to the party.

Ronpip tells everyone to calm down, rides over to the tiefling leader, and begins to measure his nub to put on a hook. The tiefling begins to pass out in shock, and Ronpip has to tourniquet his arm before he bleeds out. Antilean and Vimack begin to lecture the group on their actions and to reconsider heir lives and futures. Antilean and Vimack work together to melt down the pieces of the exploded rapier to form a hook, and Ronpip takes the parts and tinkers it together into a functional hooked prosthetic. They then tell them, once again, to make due with better lives. They even present them ideas of guarding caravans or joining a fighter's guild or joining the army.

Then one of them mentions they have a tower. Suddenly there is more interest from the party. Their leader, who says that his name is Belton Ferroph, says he does not want to give up the tower, but is conflicted with most recent events that transpired. Vimack reiterates that he will not be seeking criminal activity, and intimidates the tiefling into taking up painting, which he reluctantly agrees with. A bald headed highwaymen, who introduces himself as Gauffery, takes them off the path to the tower they used as their hide out. The tower sits just at the edge within a nearby forest, reaching just above the treeline which allowed it to be hidden behind the trees. Oddly, there does not appear to be door, but there is are a block with folded hands surrounded by eight tiles, and a keyhole between them at what could be regarded as the "front" of the tower. The tower itself looks like its made out of stone carved bricks, but Ronpip gives it an inspection and sees that it is actually a well disguised clay. Gauffery does a quick tap combination, and the folded hands on the block unfurl and pull the bottom of the tower open like drapes, opening it to the party.

Inside, they see sets of cloths and bedrolls. Vimack climbs a ladder in the center of the room to reach the second floor with Antilean following behind, where it seems like the leader stays, as well as storage for the objects they stole from various targets. Ronpip and Lorellai stays with Gauffery on the ground floor. Antilean immediately goes for the trove of stolen objects, and its apparent that based on the varying mundane objects, they stole from fairly easy targets. Together with VImack, they find:

  • Abacus
  • Block and Tackle
  • Block of incense
  • Two ten foot chains
  • box of chalk (10)
  • climber's kit
  • fish & tackle
  • manacles
  • steel mirror
  • Painter's Supplies
  • Ordinary Pot with Dirt
  • Odd Goggles

Vimack immediately sets up the Painter's Supplies, but Antilean lets him know he sent the guys off to the city for betterment, but that does not stop Vimack. He does his best to nicely arrange the paints to be welcoming to the leader. Antilean examines the goggles, and sees that they are magically enchanted with the ability to read objects. He looks through the lenses, and it seems to label objects. He puts them on and starts to see that it begins to label various objects around him. With the goggles on, he sees that the ordinary pot with dirt is labeled with question marks and offers a green outline.

Vimack climbs higher, and reaches an open air rooftop of the tower. At the center is a pedestal that appears to be a frame shape. Vimack does some heavy thinking, and remembers Yohan put his town's deed into a similar frame. Vimack calls out to Ronpip to join him with the deed to a tower they found in the sewers of Massarnae. Ronpip comes up and pulls out the deed. And splays it out into the frame, and it begins to magically fold into a paper key. Ronpip pockets the key and Vimack remembers that a keyhole was present with the folded hands outside. Antilean scoops a majority of the trove into Vimack's Bag of Holding, and they all proceed back to the ground floor. They all head outside, and Ronpip puts the key into the keyhold left in the block of folded hands. He turns the key, and the tower begins to fold in on itself from the top to the bottom, and coalesces into a single block of folded hands. The Painter's Supplies are completely destroyed, scattered across the ground as they were expelled from the folding tower. Vimack is very sad.

Ronpip picks up the single block, where the paper key has pealed away into clay, and puts it into his adventuring pack. He asks what Gauffery is going to do now, but all he gets is a blank stare. Vimack wants to have him tag along, but Ronpip is concerned he would be more problematic than worth taking. Antilean thinks it might be best to drop him off at the town they are stopping at. Ronpip asks what he's good at, and considers if he can take directions well, and Gauffery says that he takes directions very well, and thats how he got his job as a highwayman. Ronpip tells Gauffery to follow them to Phocellis and they will find him something else to do.

After some days, they find that they have ran out of rations and must hunt to have food for the continue travels. Ronpip and Vimack go out to hunt while Antilean, Gauffery, and Lorellai stay put to set up camp. They manage to get two day of rations before they need to hunt. Gauffery offers to make a stew out of the hunted finds, pulling out cooking supplies. He makes a fairly good food prep, and Ronpip offers that he should do this for a living, though Gauffery is hesitant.

As the two days pass, Vimack wants to seek Elk or Deer but he struggles on the first day. Ronpip is also foraging as they travel, and unfortunately gathers berries that are not for consumption, and consumes them along the way. While Ronpip has to stop to drop his pants, Vimack goes back out into the nearby forest, and attempts to find some local critters. He casts Speak With Animals and attempts to gather information from them. He stays low and stealthy to not startle either a fox or rabbit, who's tracks he is tracking. He spots the rabbit, and stays low and non-aggressive and distant, trying to make sure he does not scare it away. The rabbit wide eyed regards Vimack nervously. Vimack inquires about a deer, or elk. Or wolves. The rabbit nervously reacts to the word "wolves", but says there are none here. The rabbit says he knows where deer are, and points it out, but is unwilling to go himself. He points out a doe and her faun, but Vimack is hesitant. Vimack asks about other deer, not wanting to kill a mother or her child. The rabbit points out a different direction, two brothers fighting. Vimack heads in the direction the rabbit pointed out, and he finds two deer with antlers locked, insulting each other as his spell to Speak With Animals is still active. The two are insulting each other, calling each other names, and overall being a nuisance, at least to Vimack's ears. Vimack takes two shots and downs one, to the dismay of the other brother; who has not taken notice to Vimack but is hormonally bothered by his brother's deadweight on his head suddenly, vocally continuing to insult him. Vimack is very sad about this predicament, and Vimack provides mercy shots to end the last brother. Ronpip has cleared his bowels, and recollects those berries for potential use elsewhere(not eating). They camp for the night, consuming venison cooked by Gauffery.

They make it to Phocellis. Antilean recalls that his caravans have been more accepted in Phocellis compared to every other Arcaedian city or town. The city is just south of a large lake, which Lorellai has pointed out in her dream journey as the place to go for one of the Facets of Virtue, or at least that's what she believes and is otherwise not completely clear. Ronpip wants to drop him off at the Blackwood Company, though Vimack is unsure. Ronpip remembers that Phocellis has a history of magical experimentation that suddenly stopped, but verbally reassures Vimack that this place is totally fine. They take Gauffery to a Blackwood Company, and deliver him as a cook to a rather bored tenant of the facility. They try to ask him about any work nearby, but the bored tenant says that nothing really happens here, though, really he means that everyone is saying everything is always happening, but its never anything, really. He does point out theres been a very bothersome noble running around bothering folks, describes him as wearing Lavender and White Robes. The party parts ways with Gauffery and begin to investigate where the noble is in town, forgetting to ask the bored tenant of Blackwood Company.

While investigating for what they presume is Evan's brother, questioning the locals has led to each of them providing various conflicting rumours about the lake and wilds nearby. They do manage to narrow down where he is, and find Leeland Essence trying to request from the locals something that is important to him. As the party approaches, they find he is continuously demanding for a boat or at least a ride across the lake, but the local he is requesting from is adamant not to do so. They greet him with his brother's name, to Leeland's surprise, and then mention they are not only his friends but that Evan is also emperor of Arcaedia now, to Leeland's dismay. They take him to a pub to calm him down and get more information from him, finding out that the "business trip" has been a fetch quest that has been more or less unfulfillable. He has been sent to find a special herb that's located on an island in the lake, but no one seems willing to take him across the lake. Except one, which Leeland is clearly bothered by and does not wish to engage with. The party tries to understand why this particular person is not to be spoken to, but Leeland does not want to speak his name in superstitious fear that it might actually attract the person. Antilean offers to give him a ear so he can speak it quietly and he can relay it with special magical words. Leeland sighs and, as quietly as he can, shares the name of the only person with a boat willing to take him across the lake.

 Magnanimous Montgomery Mortertimer Mount Mobius Morgan But You Can Just Call Me Cahoots

Leeland explains that this is his entire name, and the man refuses to listen to anything other than the full name. Ronpip asks what he does, and Leeland replies that he is a fisherman. He is the only fisherman because no one wants to go on the lake. Vimack wonders if there is anyone else that would let them take a boat, and Leeland, exasperated, explains that he's tried everything he could. He's asked politely, he's offered money, he's offered services(tame and otherwise), and nothing has convinced any local to let him borrow a boat. Ronpip offers to go in Leeland's stead, but the entire party relays that they do not really know how to handle plants. Leeland points out that his mother provided him with a special fitting that will contain the plants and keep them fresh after picking for travel. Vimack offers to try and convince a local himself, and takes the party to the local docks of Phocellis, where he discovers there is but one boat on the docks. A painfully obvious boat. Vimack begins to question how these people live on a lake with boats and there is one fisherman in town, but realizes about putting the two and two together that Leeland is making assumptions about a lake with a dock and people must have boats given the geographical feature.

The party approaches and see a tiny, crazy gentleman working the nets, whom they can presume to be Magnanimous Montgomery Mortertimer Mount Mobius Morgan But You Can Just Call Me Cahoots. Ronpip tells Leeland to reluctantly explain what he needs, and Leeland begrudgingly explains he needs to venture across the lake to an island. The party begins to introduce themselves, to the enjoyment of Magnanimous. Ronpip asks him if anyone else in town has a boat, which Magnanimous obliges to, mentioning he has a brother who has a boat. Ronpip would like to meet him, so Magnanimous goes to an alleyway, puts on a hat, and adds Junior to his name with a loud and extended inflection. Leeland is beside himself with clear annoyance, and complains to the party that this is exactly why he did not want to speak with this psychotic person. The party tries to have a conversation with him and convince him to help them to an island on the lake, and it goes about as well as it would seem, with Magnanimous changing the subject often, or speaking in conflicting information and making implications. He asks what the party would trade for his boat service, and Ronpip offers him a bottle of Thimbleville Mead, which he quickly downs. Appreciating the drink, he tells the boys to get on his boat, and they begin to sail off into the mists of the lake and into the unknown.
