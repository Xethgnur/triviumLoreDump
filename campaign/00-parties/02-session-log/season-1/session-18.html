Session 18

Beginning after Session 17, we get a recalled memory of Ronpip, who snuck off from the party to complete some untouched business that has been bothering him since his brother was stabbed in the soup kitchen. He finds himself on a busy street, in front of a Leatherback Tunn. Ronpip enters, and the host greets Ronpip by name. Ronpip is momentarily surprised, but the host clarifies that he is always welcome to a Leatherback Tunn, per "his" orders. Ronpip requests to speak with The Beak. The host hesitates, but seems like he is compelled to take Ronpip to a secreted room behind the bar. Ronpip is taken to a Greeting Room, where Ronpip is taken to a table with two seats. Ronpip waits exactly four minutes, before The Beak appears from the shadows and sits at the table with Ronpip, with two pints of mead in his hands. Ronpip takes notice that there is a gash across his left eye, which he did not see before. Ronpip and him have a discussion about recent events, Ronpip pointing out that The Beak clearly does not want wanton murder. He offers The Beak that he will promise to stay out of The Beak's business ventures, and The Beak states that Ronpip may occasionally get a letter if they are getting too close to breaking this promise. Ronpip agrees to the terms, and walks out of the Leatherback Tunn, unimpeded or bothered. He picks up some wax paper and ink, and returns to his party.

The party begins their sail through Lake Ozsmark sailing on Magnanimous Montgomery Mortertimer Mount Mobius Morgan But You Can Just Call Me Cahoots's boat, named after himself, but a boat. Magnanimous is pushing the boat along the lake with a long oar. Vimack offers to push the boat, and he gives it a good boost through the waters, catching Leeland off balance with the sudden jerk of speed. A fog wall encroaches in front of them and is making it hard navigate further, not to be helped by Leeland's lack of direction to the island he is looking for. Vimack stops the boat just before they reach the fog wall, and Ronpip sees through the Tool of War that the fog wall is, in fact, magical in nature. Despite, they pass into the fog wall, the smell of ozone filling their noses and Vimack noticing his sense of North is significantly weakened. With no immediate direction, Magnanimous barefoot and barehanded climbs his mast in order to see if he can see anything above the fog. He spots an island and points it out. Vimack pushes the boat towards the direction Magnanimous points out, and Antilean climbs the mast to confirm Magnanimous' view, and tilts the boat dangerously as Vimack tries to turn it. Antilean cannot see anything, and he returns back down. As Vimack continues to be guided by Magnanimous, the mist opens, and a small pocket of open air surrounds an small, half-mile wide island with trees on it. Vimack notices birds in the trees but they are not making noise. Ronpip casts Detect Magic, and manages to concentrate through the myriad of colors to see that it gives off a large aura of illusion. Vimack wants to see what the island is about but Ronpip is worried it might be a crab. Vimack and Antilean lob things at the island, but no sound seems to be coming. Vimack tries to peer through the trees when Leeland asks if there are flowers, but Vimack cannot see any. They beach the island, no sound of the water dispersing, and cannot hear each other speaking. Vimack shoves his head into the water out curiosity, and discovers it did not change the sound. The party does their best to communicate non-verbally, as all words spoken seem to be extremely muffled. They determine how well they can communicate, and explore inwards to the center of the island. As they approach the center clearing, they do not spot these blue flowers Leeland is searching for and Ronpip does not witness anything odd or strange about the environment, other than the field of illusory magic that seems to mute all sounds. The party returns to the boat, and realize they are unaffected once they get off the beach.

Vimack pushes the boat East-ish, back into the fog, and soon discover another island that initially appears exactly as the one before. Antilean and Vimack spot that there are animals on the island, and they seem to be shunting and appearing back a short distance. Vimack beaches the boats. The party checks if they can hear each other, and it seems this island does not have the same deafening silence radiating from it. Leeland notices the animals teleporting about, and wishes he could have a stew from those animals. Vimack is disappointed, since this sounds so closely like Evan. Vimack steps off the boat, and the moment he touches the sand, he is teleported 20ft on to the island. While Vimack is discovering how the antics of the island teleportation works, Antilean asks Leeland about the Blue Flowers and what they are for. Leeland relays that they are for a special perfume his mother intends to make, but offers no details. Antilean is suspicious but does not press further. Vimack, 20ft into the island, tries to see if there are blue flowers on the island, and Dorizoul follows Vimack on to the island, teleporting to his feet. Vimakc tells her to sit, and calls out to Ronpip for rope, which he then ties to himself while Ronpip ties the other end to the boat. Vimack carefully picks up Dorizoul and proceeds further into the island. The sudden blips of movement that Vimack is making is disorienting, and Vimack ends up rope lining himself from the amount of movement, and Dorizoul is tossed from his hands. Vimack is distraught, knowing if he moves or she moves, they will end up teleporting a distance instead of being able to move directly to each other. Ronpip, concerned about his brother since the rope was tot for a moment, he leaves the boat and ends up teleporting 10ft on to the island. Antilean is concerned about Vimack and Ronpip now, and hesitates about entering the island. Ronpip tests the jaunting, and follows the rope as best as he can while navigating the island and the movement teleportation. Vimack can see Ronpip teleporting around nearby, but is still trying to formulate a plan. Ronpip turns his Tool of War into a Javelin to get himself around the island. Vimack rages and tries to get his own lightning to strike himself, but it instead targets a beetle that flies too close. Ronpip javelins himself to his brother, which lands at Vimack's feet, and arrives at his feet. Ronpip tries to push Vimack to get him to reach Dorizoul but causes them both to teleport, VImack a little farther than himsef, causing Vimack to teleport passed Dorizoul. Vimack tells Dorizoul to go with Ronpip, as Ronpip within touch. Ronpip turns his Tool of War into a Lance and reaches out to Vimack. Vimack takes the lance, and Ronpip pulls. Vimack teleports 10ft behind Ronpip.

Meanwhile, Leeland is beginning to get concerned at how long Vimack and Ronpip have been out on the island. Antilean is hesitant to leave the ship considering the effects of the island. He fires a crossbow bolt at a nearby tree, and nails it perfectly where he intends to, surprised that the bolt did not teleport. The rope goes slack, and Antilean begins to pull the rope in a hurry. Ronpip ties the rope to himself and teleports himself back towards the boat to untangle the rope. He lets Antilean and Leeland know they are okay but working out some problems. He makes his way back to Vimack and Dorizoul. With some complex planning and maneuvering and utility Tool of War useage, the group manages to get the three together, and Ronpip and Vimack are met with futility in their plans and Ronpip calls out to Antilean to "reel them in". Vimack does not want to go because that means leaving Dorizoul behind in his eyes, but Antilean pulls anyways. He initially fails dramatically, not being able to pull anything, but then he injects himself with Bull's Strength, buffing out temporarily and yanking Vimack to the ground as he teleports forward, and Antilean momentarily falls. Antilean continues to pull and Vimack anchors himself down not wanting to go. After some brotherly arguing, they all simply move and teleport back to the boat, and leave as soon as possible.

They reach the next island, and find a rocky outcropping at the center with a tunnel. Antilean volunteers to hop off the boat first and enter the island's vicinity. He lands in the sand, and nothing happens. Ronpip joins him as Antilean asks what kind of magic might be surrounding the place. Ronpip uses Detect Magic and detects Conjuration magic, which unerves Antilean. Vimack tells Dorizoul to sit and protect the boat before joining his comrades. They walk into the island and enter the tunnel. Lorellai seems excited to adventure into the tunnel, until they reach an corner. Ronpip prepares himself, and around the corner, he spots a Vulture, a Sea Hawk, a Bear, and a bipedal lizard, who are all wearing armor. Ronpip reports back that its strange company. Lorellai wants to see and goes around the corner, everyone else draws weapons. Upon seeing the party, the bipedal lizard responds with its name and a series of titles, and the party recognizes them, sort of. Ronpip laughs and points out the idiots got polymorphed, which then makes the bear stand on two legs and begin to frustratedly lecture him. They have a small conversation with each other's situations, and the bipedal lizard provides the party with a little inspirational praise from Bahamut, and the two part. The party exits the tunnel, only to find themselves on a similar island, with Magnanimous' Boat still ashore with Leeland, who is looking extra perturbed. Leeland vocalizes his disappointment with not having found these blue flowers he was sent for, and the party mirrors these feelings.

They pass by several other islands with their own various quirks. One in particular has an actual inhabitant, who is out hammering a sign that says NO ENTER on it. A minute passes by as they watch him and approach, and the sign disappears, as does the man. And a moment later, it repeats. The party thinks of ways to help, but he tells them he's tried most of them and all have failed, and for them to go out to save themselves.

They keep scouring islands in pockets of of the obscuring mist, and eventually reach a treeless island that has a ancient ruins that are surrounded by a unique blue patch of flowers. There appears to be four pillars of lapis lazuli, with a depression between them implying an entrance. Ronpip surprisingly does not detect the same radical magics that the previous islands had. Lorellai is excited and hyping herself up as this appears to be the place she needed to be. Leeland is also expressing excitement because those appear to be the flowers he was looking for.

Vimack expresses deep concern and nervousness, and takes careful steps, expressing the safety that comes with the steps that show nothing happening. Ronpip shares they must destroy EVERYTHING on the island to ensure it is safe, which triggers Leeland. But Ronpip admits he is fucking with him, and runs off on to the island. Vimack is perturbed. Not wanting to lose track of Leeland while they explore, they ask Magnanimous to keep an eye on Leeland.

The party approaches the pillars of blue stone, and water from the lake lifts and collides over them to form a large fish like being. She gestures aggressively for the party to stop. The Party are on edge, but take no action. She tells them to answer her riddle, or be banished. Temporarily. She says:

I am soft to touch, yet I cleave through the earth I move with the winds, and can rip you from your feet But if I remain still, I can fill you with mirth. What am I?

The party correctly guesses that the answer is: Water. The guardian nods and the door opens, leading deeper into the island. Lorellai speaks Aquan to the guardian, and then follows the party within.

Inside the area, the party completes several water themed puzzles, such as a deep pool of water requiring a person to swim below a normally infeasible depth and drain the water to reveal the next entrance, a series of orbs that perform tricks based on the phases of water, and a watery gate that is passible only when blood slows the mechanisms down.

At the end of the path through this dungeon-like area, the party finds themselves in a sanctum like area. Shimmering windows between pillars that hold the domed ceiling up look into the Plane of Water, a vast ocean of alien creatures and enormous corals and endless schools of fish. At the center of of the chamber is a sapphic crystal embedded into a geode of blue stone that sits upon a fountain. Lorellai walks to the fountain and reaches out for the crystal. Vimack stops her, and asks how she knows its okay to do this. Lorellai responds with confidence that it will be okay, and touches the crystal.

From below, two leviathan sized water elementals rise like water spouts from below. One seems warm, semi-green and soft in features, while the other is a cold cyan with crystals of ice sharpening its aggressive features. Lorellai turns to the party and confesses that she has not been truthful to the party. She confesses she was raised in the Plane of Water, and did not want "The First Tear" to fall into the wrong hands, and defied her mother and father to ensure it was given to worthy heroes. Her parents being the two water elementals before her.

The two elementals demand a trial to find if the party is worthy, and test the party's integrity, bravery, and will with waves of rushing water, ice, sleet, snow, and other trials by water. The party does everything in their power to maintain their control and safety with each other, and take notice that the two elementals do not actually attack the party. As such, the party does not attack the two elementals, though Antilean made a pre-emptive shot in fear. When the party gathers around each other as each wave bashes against the party, he asks Lorellai to apologize to her father on his behalf.

The party succeeds in their team work, and are offered "The First Tear", a special magic item associated with the Plane of Water. Lorellai unfortunately mentions that she cannot continue to travel with them, and must return home. The party is reluctant, but give her hugs and parting words while she returns with her elemental parents back to her home world. The arty leaves, and returns the surface. Antilean is taken aback by the small venture, as it was never his intention to become an adventurer. Meanwhile, Leeland has collected many blue flowers off the island. Magnanimous has been napping for a majority of the time. The party carefully returns to the boat to not disturb Magnanimous, and take off. Oddly, the mist parts for them, creating a path through to return them back to the docks of Phocellis.

On shore, the party parts with Magnanimous by giving him a net full of knocked out fish via Ronpip's Eldritch Blast. Leeland and Antilean put palms to their faces. Leeland wishes to return home, but the party decides they will escort him back, but after they complete a bit of business endeavours for Antilean, and tell him to stay at a local inn until then.
