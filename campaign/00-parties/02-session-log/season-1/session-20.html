Session 20

Following Session 19, Ronpip Thimble-bimble, Antilean Faraday, and Vimack Badger wait in anticipation, knowing the Elven Army is nigh about to approach. But, just as Hysseld predicted, as soon as the first snowflake fell over the the wall, the army crossed the forest. They see that Druids of Winter have changed the weather, forced snow clouds to converge and cover the area, as a blast of frigid cold air rushes to the city, through the northern town, up the defensive north wall, and across the targeted city.

Antilean hangs back, staying in a sniper tower and tries to provide support as best as he can. Vimack chooses to run with the Vanguard, as does Ronpip with Horsemen. Together, with the combined forces of Elven forces, Treants , and the forward armies of Vasaaros, they prepare their defense and counter attack.

From the forest where a snowblind strikes against the northern front, a rushing swarm of undead break through the treeline, hollering in bloodlust as they rush through the empty farmlands. Ronpip, from his experience in large scale battles, notices that the zombies are not the only thing charging in. Behind them, packs of armored dogs are racing behind their heels, though their target is obviously not the undead they trail. Trailing the war hounds, cultist war champions run behind them. However, the High Elves remain in formation at the far end of the battle field and do not charge forward. Vimack can make out among the High Elves their leader, adorned in golden armor and a blood red cape, and Priestess Yurreina Paramoure on either side of the rear guard, with Druids of Winter performing a ritual between them.

As the pack of zombies rush forward, Arcaedian archers fire in formation, aiming to lower their numbers before the vanguard have to meet them in melee combat. Trebuchets fire in tandem, with the West Trebuchets firing upon the druids performing a Winter Rite. Snow and Dirt blast into the air on impact of the payloads, and zombies fall to some of the volleys of arrows. The Druids finish a portion of their Rite, and a blast of flash frozen air crash into the Arcaedian defense.

Antilean preps his Invisibility Seeing device, which takes a hot minute to set up, but sees nothing that would have escaped his normal vision. Vimack and Ronpip charge in against the zombie horde with their respective squadrons. The Treants awaken, drive their roots into the earth and awaken nearby trees to joins the fight. Warhounds that weave between the zombies go for Ronpip, who attempts to parry their attacks with his skill with his greatsword. Antilean releases Dragoon to join the fray and help while he remains at the tower. With his heigh advantage, he picks off important targets at an incredible distance.

Suddenly, a plan comes to his mind, and he tells the archers around him to just keep shooting, while he slides down the tower wall with his claws and proceeds forth into the vanguard, whistling for Dragoon to return to him.

The fight draws out for several minutes; with neither side seeming to budge. The defense at the wall holds, despite the blasts of winter winds buffeting the soldiers and a tireless, undead enemy rushing in first. But the trio manages to keep morale high, charging into danger and keeping the legions at bay. The bear warriors that came to assist the defense, and morph themselves into werebears, pushing the charging undead back with the forces of nature and claws and wiping out the unit facing Vimack's squadron.

The trebuchets adjust, and focus upon the druids, lobbing their payload at them. The boulders impact around them, killing most of heir group. The lead druid maintains her concentration despite the screams and crashes, but the moment she opens her eyes, and sees she is the last one alive, her courage breaks and she attempts to flee the battlefield.

The Treants crash through the remaining enemy vanguard and the bear fighters clean up the remaining hounds that attacked Ronpip's horsemen among the horde of zombies. One Treant even pulls a large rock from the lake nearby and chucks it into the enemy. While the defense are being worn down by the zombie hordes, the enemy high elven archers finally take their action and fire upon the vanguard with no head for their own zombie units. Their arrows, despite being a large volley, are deadly accurate and pin down the defensive units.

Vimack is an unstoppable machine, tearing through individuals like they are nothing but training dummies, and giving his squadron increased moral and hype, and they follow through with as much energy as they can to match Vimack's seemingly endless rage and lightning.

Ronpip leads the charge on the otherside, using his horsemen to pin the war hounds and war champions from their flank by encroaching them like a whirlpool of blades and horses, whittling them away as they box them into death. Antilean rushes to Ronpip's side, helping pick off targets with Dragoon and healing Ronpip with a needle of healing liquids. Vimack continues to charge, despite the volleys of arrows, uncaring of the damage he is taking, but does not notice his unit is also taking some hits and dying to the barrage. Despite, Vimack gives another doubled down war cry, bringing attention to himself to get his units to charge with him into the enemy elven army headlong.

Priestess Yurreina Paramoure attempts to take control of Ronpip, but Ronpip's natural resistance to magic prevents her from taking his mind. Bothered, she attempts to target him directly, which catches Ronpip's attention. Ronpip warns Antilean that magic will not work with her, flips her the bird, and charges directly into her. Ronpip's sword hand waivers and tingles, but he strikes true, using the distance of his lance to maintain distance. He strikes her once with a charge and strikes her again with a thrust, which narrowly misses as Yurreina parries it away but with Ronpip's knowledge of fighting and the Tool of War's influence, rears the horse around to ensure the strike finds its purchase. Antilean, from a distance, assists Ronpip and takes shots at Yurreina between Ronpip's strikes, blasting portions of her armor off.

The enemy elven army attempt to barracade againt the horemen and Vimack. The horsemen charge with reckless abandon, weakening the wall as Vimack charges in with the same force as four more horses concentrated into a single being.

But, as soon as Vimack breaks into the army, magic drives itself into his mind and heart, and lost in his rage, he falls prey to mind control. Suddenly, a once inspiring ally has become a most fearsome foe, as Vimack turns on his own men without concern or notice.

Yurreina attempts to dismount and strike Ronpip, but she is still shellshocked and her body does not respond as quickly as she would like and she misses terribly. Her anger only rises. A strange sound comes from the sky, but none of the party notices anything strange.

Vimack hears a command in his own mind to attack his comrades; the sensation is foreign enough that Vimack, while not able to stop his action, is able to hold back enough from killing his own comrades. In realization, Vimack frees his mind from control and focuses on Elwin Petyra, the enemy elven leader and the one who tried to force Vimack to fight his own allies.

Ronpip shifts his lance into a great sword once more, and prepares a might swing the sunder the hammer from Yurreina's arms. Ronpip feints a lance thrust, but instead leaps from his mount and throws off his shield, and comes down swinging hard upon Yurreina. She attempts to block in reaction and keep her weapon in hand. Blinding white-blue sparks emit between the bite of the great sword and the warhammer, slicing it in half. The Tool of War feels stronger in Ronpip's hand, as though it had been rejuvenated in an eldritch way. Ronpip takes the opportunity from his battle high to make a second attack. Yurreina is taking aback that she quick steps back in retreat, but Ronpip rallies himself and continues to put pressure upon her, striking twice more, cutting through her armor and seeming slicing straight through the armor's enchatment. Antilean and Dragoon continue to follow up with shots between Ronpip's attacks, continuing to pick off chunks of her armor as Ronpip slices into the openings. She begins to puke blood as her body is beginning to give out.

Vimack's group is winded from chasing Vimack and formerly fighting zombies, and are starting to falter. Some of the archers fire at Vimack with deadly accuracy, noticing that their leader's control has been broken from him.

Critical Wound!

One strikes straight into Vimack's arm and causes him to drop one of this swords from its force, his arm damaged to the point of unable to wield a weapon.

Petyra attempts to cast Hold Person upon him, and Vimack manages to rip the arcane chains from the Weave from the ground and prevents himself from being held down by magic.

Yurreina staggers, holding a hold in her stomach, and begins to chant a divine incantation. A halo of red light comes over her as her chanting reaches a crescendo. And the sound of a whistling object from the sky goes from a whisper to a scream and impacts directly upon Yurreina with the force of a trebuchet payload. But, Antilean and Ronpip do not see a large stone payload, and instead see a shell of metal, from which four metal automatons step out from. From the sky, an airship comes out from the cloud line, and Ronpip recognizes it as the Thumbleville Airbrigade bombarding the landscape with artillery from the sky. The Automatons are dropped straight on top of enemy armies, who then proceed to assist in the defense of Vasaaros.

A rope comes down from the sky near Ronpip, and greets Ronpip upon the battlefield. More gnomes drop down from the skyships above. Petyra is caught aghast and confused and angry and disappointed and frightened. He then spots Vimack making his way directly to him and all of his emotions conspire into a single primal fear for his own life. Vimack contemplates taking him on directly, but realizes he has an item that has the chance to end the fight sooner than if he were to attack head on with one arm and heavily wounded trying to reach Petyra. He pulls out The Talisman of Pure Good and invokes its spell towards him. A fissure to hell opens up, and hellfire chains rip through to pierce through him. In pain and shock, he turns into mist and tries to escape. But in pure irony, more chains explode from the fissure, enrapture the mist and drag it into the fissure, straight to the hells. The fissure closes and all thats left is a sliver of melted snow and the stench of brimstone that disappears along the wind. Petyra is gone, taken to the hells for whatever torturous unlife awaits him. The rest of the enemy elves begin to falter, seeing their leader has fallen and others seeing their comrades die to metal humanoids who do not tire and do not fear and do not have morals, they drop their weapons and flee. War champions of Yurreina are shot down with mirad of bolts, the dogs are put down to enternal sleep.

Vimack picks up his swords, sheathing one to pick his sundered one with his good hand. Antilean celebrated the end of the battle with gunshots into the air. Ronpip takes a trophy of Yurreina's head, or what is left of it from the impact.

And the party returns to the city with the rest of their new found comrades...
