Amun-Ra

Introduction

The Amun people are expressive and emotionally driven, and tend to be very specialized in what they know and do. This has been their way for ages, as they are unchallenged masters sailors of sea and sand. The Amun people are avid magical relic hunters, seeking the greatest payout they can no matter the cost to the recipient. They are hard working people who never question the difficulty of a task so long as the reward is generous, and are willing to take immense risk if it doubles their gains.

History

The established history of pre-Amun founding is all but lost to the sandsea and time. Records from unreliably chaotic archeological discoveries and exceedingly scarce written materials, pre-Amun depictions regarded the entire continent a magical and technical paradise; something greater than the paramount of a golden age. Vague descriptions include pictures of cities of mirrors and glass the size and height of ancient forests; technology able to combat any curse, disease, and even aging; control over beasts from other realms and dimensions. But what had caused its disappearance remains a dauntlessly endless mystery...

The Amun people do not know how long their ruler has sat within his palace and no one has seen this ruler in person and came back the same as they were, but his presence is felt throughout the nation. Otherwise, often the person who has obtained the most relics is often in charge of smaller, indiscernible districts of towns or caravans. While some are artisanal leaders, others are part of organized crime. And the only ones above them are their Godking.

Economy

The Amun have a monopoly on glassmaking, spices, "Dune Spice", special woven fabrics found only in their particular region, and rare minerals that only come from the wastes and sandsea. They also export and import exotic animals of which they catch from various places. As a food source, they rely on the fruits and fish that they gather from the seaside or along the few oases, if not also sparse animal herding. The Amun people prefer to haggle, finding a middle ground over time through pressure and tactical charisma. Prices start high, bids rebuttal low, and through perfect negotiation, both come out on top.

Most Amun are artisans and traders, attempting to accumulate enough wealth to lift up their social status. If an Amun is not up for trade work, relic hunting through the seas and/or dunes can bring about a large sum of money and reputation. Some might even say the country subsides on piracy and graverobbery.

Diplomacy

Amun-Ra has thin diplomatic relations with other territories, at best. The scarlet corsair pirates that arise from the kingdom are always a note of concern, though the Amun Theocracy will always declare no relation to the pirates, nor any real intention to stop them. Trade relationships are often the best between Amun-Ra and other countries. Their ships are often the fastest to sail, the merchants have fear of nothing at sea, and they will trade nearly anything, for the right price.

The Amun also have relations with the nomadic tribes of the sandsea, often hiring them as guides, messengers, or mercenaries.

Military

The Amun are masters at marine warfare, and unconventional land warfare. 

The Weave Wind Ships of the Amun are fast, and extremely maneuverable, utilizing the winds with the highest proficiency with a mixture of careful, culturally shared engineering and very specialized training, with a hint of Arcane Weave mixed into the clothe of the sails. Weave Wind Warships seen on the horizon are a fight not worth fighting, as they will circle your ship far sooner than you could get a broadside off. Scarlet Sails seen on the horizon are a death sentence.

On Land, the Amun utilize animals to their advantage. Swarms of war carrions, legions of mounted elephants, Camel Caravans, armored hyenas, and furthermore to the imagination. It is not uncommon for many to summon creatures from other realms to fight for them, or to raise the dead.

Arcane Capabilities

The Amun-Rae are not known to have Arcane power, and it is very rare to see any who present a talent for it. Those that are born with such powers are more than often forced into servitude, never learning more than what they naturally discover. It is not unheard of for high noble citizens to flaunt their "indentured" magic users by making them cast their magic on mundane tasks. Even if Amun Magic Users did develop their magic skills, their powers would be severally diminished due to the volatile weave in Amun-Ra. Wizards and Sorcerers from other nations scarcely visit Amun-Ra, more apt to send others to find the relics of ancient times for them.

Since long before written history, something happened in Amun-Ra's past that has caused the area to be incapable of developing magic on its own. Prolonged magic dies out sooner than it should be intended, rituals fail often unless sources are kept close and constant, and in some areas of the sandsea may cause chaotic eruptions of magic from the spellcaster.
